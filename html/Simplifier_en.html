Optimize an Information System

The technical capabilities of the Cloud allow infinite scalability. And that’s good, since data sources have multiplied, as have users and uses case business.
Unfortunately, there are side effects: the systems are no longer intelligible, maintainable and they end up being expensive.
We offer features to detect dead matter in systems, whether tables, files, dashboards, in order to bring these systems back to their optimum.

Detect Dead material in feed streams

The problem

On average, 50% of what is stored in databases has no added value. These are countless “dead branches” composed of code, tables, views, files, which are wrongly maintained in the systems with a considerable impact: system inertia, maintenance costs, unsustainable Cloud bills and high environmental impacts (GHG).

Our solutions

Thanks to an analysis of information uses, associated with the information flow (data lineage), {openAudit} identifies unnecessary flows and the “data points” associated with them (tables / files). This is what we call dead branches.

• {openAudit} analyzes audit database logs and the data visualization layer to discover what data is actually used.

• From the fields used in the databases to supply data visualization tools, or ad hoc queries (ODBC, JDBC), or from specific ETL/ELT flows, {openAudit} identifies the information flows, the tables which are in source, that is to say the "living branches" of the information system.

• In contrast, {openAudit} identifies "dead branches", i.e. tables, procedures, ETL/ELT jobs which construct useless information.

• {openAudit} implements these analyzes dynamically, and thus allows, by creating a significant depth of history, to formally identify the branches which are continually unused, with everything they concentrate: tables, files, procedures, ELT/ETL jobs. Mass decommissioning can take place in record time.

• In the Cloud, via an analysis of certain logs, {openAudit} identifies the cost of keeping dead branches in the system. The saveable machine resource is also highlighted. The company can adopt a FinOps and GreenOps logic.



Lower Cloud costs! Identify and remove “dead branches”

{openAudit} can be used to significantly reduce Cloud bills since on average 50% of what is found there is not used.

Reduce IT debt - why do it ?

{openAudit} can help reduce “IT debt”: identify “dead material” to decommission it to make the systems read intelligible, easier to migrate.

Detect obsolete, replicated Dashboards

The problem

The data visualization layer has often been placed in the hands of the business.

For good and not so good reasons, the number of dashboards has tended to proliferate. Indeed, user teams naturally tend to replicate dashboards, often with minor nuances for often very occasional use. And everything that has been created remains on the platforms indefinitely, creating cohorts of replicated or obsolete dashboards.

Our solutions

We have developed solutions to streamline dataviz platforms in an extremely efficient manner. {openAudit} will analyze the sources, the queries, the expressions, the variables, the data finally displayed, to allow optimizations.

Obsolete, broken, replicated dashboards are detected and can be archived or discarded to reduce costs and reduce the risk of errors.

Technically



• {openAudit} will directly parse the files of the data visualization solution to recover the intelligence, the structure of the dashboards and the semantic layer if there is one.

• {openAudit} will also access the repository to keep the consistency of IDs between the different dashboard objects (semantic layer, query, dashboard, others).

• An {openAudit} probe will retrieve certain logs from the audit databases which are associated with the data visualization solutions.

• {openAudit} will compare the dashboards with each other and detect replication based on different criteria

Simply over-administer an SAP BO platform

Specific functionalities for SAP BO have been developed allowing massive archiving actions of replicated, obsolete, dysfunctional reports. Which is typically essential when preparing for a migration.
